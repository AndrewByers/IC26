Who it's for

Built for the founder / CEO who wants to maximise the enterprise value of their business

You've spent years supporting customers, running products, teams or a P&L inside a larger organisation. You don't want to see that value deminish or be undervalued. You want to see the business scale to the next level and to oversee direction of travel.

The obstacle is rarely ambition. It's sequencing: how to build beyond where you are now, have the team, technology and traction that attracts higher multiples and continues to build on your legacy. This engagement is the sequencing.

  • 01You're a founder / owner — Director or CEO.
  • 02You're drawn to maximising enterprise value, and achieve a positive exit.
  • 03You want a staged, de-risked path — not more of the same and hope.
  • 04You'll commit to a focused six-month cycle.
The approach

One engagement, two tracks

The work runs on two tracks at once. Neither reaches the outcome alone.

Track 01 — Stability

The shield

Identify the gap between the current position and where you see the business in the future, carries genuine responsibility and leaves you the bandwidth to work on the business, not in the business. Stability isn't the compromise — it's the platform everything else stands on.

Track 02 — Ownership

The build

Move from constant operational pressure to a more strategic Chairman role making the business non-dependant on your day-to-day involvement. Deliberate, evidenced steps toward a business you'll scale and sell.

The stability track progress and shields the ownership track. That is the whole idea behind A → C → B.

The framework

The six-month operating cycle

Every engagement follows the same disciplined arc — from scattered ideas to a signed Letter of Intent. One focused theme a month.

Month 01

Foundation & Governance

Clear the constraints in your current position and turn fragmented research into one formal scale deck.

Month 02

Thesis & Identity

Harden the sector thesis, and build the senior team that keeps your corporate footing secure while you scale.

Month 03

Team & Financing

Build a genuine leadership team framework - identifying internal and external candidates — and address friction points within the current business.

Month 04

Risk & Diligence

Develop a structured due-diligence checklist and a clear framework for you the founder's transition out of the day-to-day business.

Month 05

Re-Engineer & Innovation

Open real conversations within the business and identify the real customer journey and the systems, processes and KPI's that really matter to your business.

Month 06

Diagnostic & Execution

Finalise the Strategy and align the team for the new world, with you in the Chairman role.

The method

Why A → C → B

Most business exits fail because founders try to jump straight from A — where you are now — to B — outcome you want to achieve. It's a leap taken from a standing start, usually under financial pressure.

A → C → B inserts C on purpose: a stabilised, shielded framework that gives you time, cashflow and standing to build properly. You negotiate from strength, not desperation.

The framework and tools behind the work are proprietary to the practice. What you build with them — your strategy, your market map, your diligence checklist — is yours to keep.
A

Your present

A founder / owner / CEO in your own organisation.

C

The bridge

A stabilised, shielded position — time, cashflow, the team and technologies to build from.

B

Your stated outcome (maximum enterprise value for the business you built)

A company you've built, scaled properly with a dedicated leadership team attracting the best offers.
